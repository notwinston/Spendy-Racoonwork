Social Feature Guideline
Mascot — The Raccoon
The raccoon is the heart of the app. Its design is simplified, similar to Duolingo's art style, and evolves visually as the user levels up — making progression feel personal and rewarding.
Design Evolution (by level)
The raccoon's appearance changes across five stages, reflecting the user's growing financial discipline:
Messy Raccoon — Ragged-looking, wearing a grey t-shirt. Just getting started.
Backpack Raccoon — Navy blue and white bomber jacket with a backpack. Building habits.
Clean Raccoon — Blue turtleneck sweater, glasses, and black trousers. Reliably disciplined.
Sunglasses Raccoon — Red Hawaiian shirt, light blue beach shorts, sunglasses, and sunscreen lines under the eyes. Mastery unlocked.
CEO Raccoon — Black suit, black shades. Long-term, sustained financial discipline.
Emotional States
The raccoon's expression shifts based on the user's financial health score, providing real-time feedback:
Happy — Health score is high and stable.
Concerned — Nearing a budget limit or showing an overspending trend.
Angry — Budget has been broken.
Celebrating — A financial goal has been reached.
Interaction
Tapping the raccoon prompts it to suggest a personalised financial optimisation tip.

Leveling Up the Raccoon
The raccoon evolves by accumulating XP through consistent financial behaviour. Each evolution stage has an XP threshold that must be met alongside minimum streak and quest requirements — so you can't brute-force an evolution without actually building good habits.
Evolution Thresholds
Each stage requires a combination of cumulative XP, streak consistency (percentage of weeks with at least one quest completed), and total quests completed. All three must be met — hitting the XP target alone is not enough.

Evolution Moment
When the raccoon evolves, it's treated as a milestone — a short animation plays, the new form is revealed, and the user receives a celebratory nudge (e.g., a badge, a bonus XP reward, or a special tip from the raccoon in its new form). Friends can see the evolution reflected on your profile, creating a natural social moment.
Keeping Your Raccoon's Level
The raccoon's evolution stage does not reset — once earned, a form is permanent. However, the raccoon's emotional state still fluctuates week to week based on financial health, so even a CEO Raccoon can look concerned or angry if budgeting slips. The form reflects your history; the emotion reflects your present.



Reward System
The primary reward is XP, which drives raccoon progression and rank advancement. XP is earned through:
Logging every expense for the week
Staying under a budget category
Hitting a savings goal milestone
Completing a hard quest (budget challenges, no-spend days, etc.)
Logging in and reviewing your spending summary
Smart Rewards
Smart Rewards passively detects when you spend at partner merchants and occasionally surprises you with cashback or discounts — no activation needed. Rewards are triggered based on budgeting behaviour, so spending smart feels even better. It's not about spending more; it's about getting a little something back when you were already going to.


Quest System
Daily Quests reset every day and are personalised to your habits. They are easy or medium difficulty — habit-building, low-friction tasks designed to make logging and budgeting feel routine.
Examples: Log all your expenses today / Stay under your coffee budget today / Review your spending summary.
Rewards: XP.
Weekly Quests reset every Monday and are personalised to your habits. They are medium or hard difficulty — the challenge layer, requiring sustained discipline over several days.
Examples: Stay under your total food budget for the full week / Complete a no-spend day 3 times this week / Hit your savings contribution goal this week.
Rewards: XP.
Solo vs. Group Quests
Some quests are solo by default. Group quests are a separate variant where you and friends tackle the same challenge together — everyone must complete their individual version for the group to succeed. Group completion grants a bonus XP reward on top of the base, making cooperation genuinely worthwhile without punishing anyone if a friend drops off.
Personalisation Logic
Quests scale to what's realistic for each user's budget. "Stay under $50 on dining" shouldn't apply the same way to someone budgeting $200 vs. $80 on food. Quests reference the user's own categories and thresholds so every challenge feels attainable but not trivial.

Rank System
Ranks reflect cumulative financial discipline over time — not a weekly leaderboard snapshot. Progression is based on three combined factors: total XP earned, streak consistency, and quests completed. All three must grow together; you can't grind XP alone to skip ahead.

Progression Formula
Each rank has a threshold requiring a minimum across all three metrics. XP is cumulative and never resets. Streak consistency is measured as the percentage of weeks where the user completed at least one quest — missing a single day is not harshly penalised. Quest count is the total completed, weighted slightly heavier toward hard quests.
How Ranks Are Displayed
The rank name and a small icon sit to the right of the user's username as a tag, visible to friends. It's the first thing someone sees when visiting a profile.

Badges System
Badges tell a story about a user's financial journey rather than functioning as a forgettable onboarding checklist. Target: 20 well-designed badges across three categories.
Consistency Badges — e.g., "Logged every expense for 4 weeks straight," "Under budget 3 months in a row."
Milestone Badges — e.g., "Hit your first savings goal," "Completed 10 hard quests."
Raccoon Badges — e.g., "Evolved your raccoon for the first time," "Reached CEO Raccoon."
